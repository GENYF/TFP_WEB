Congnitive-Behavioral Therapy

Cognitive-Behavior programs are used to treat social phobia and include four components.

  1. EXPLAIN: The fear of social phobia is usually learned, and there are methods to unlearn such fear.
  2. LEARN AND SUBSTITUTE: People learn new social skills and replace negative thoughts with positive ones.
  3. EXPOSE: People are gradually exposed (in imagination and in reality) to social situations such as conversations and presentations.
  4. PRACTICE: People get used to conversations or presentations by repeating and practicing the second and third steps.

About 30-80 percent of those who have completed the program have reduced social fears.

Drug Therapy

Drug therapy is to treat social phobia by prescribing drugs such as tranquilizers and antidepressants by psychiatrists. However, there are two problems with drug treatment.

  1. About 50-75% of individuals relapse when they stop taking the drugs.
  2. Long-term drug treatment can have serious side effects, such as memory loss.

Conclusion

The results of Congnitive-Behavioral Therapy and Drug Therapy are similar. Congnitive-Behavioral Therapy, however, has the advantage of having no side effects.
